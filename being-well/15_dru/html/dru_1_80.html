Continue on for a list of ideas to help minimise the harm if you, or a friend, consumes drugs. Then consider the question.

 Mindset: Consider your mood and feelings before deciding whether to take a drug Setting: Think about the physical space where you or your friends will be under the influence of drugs Research: Investigate the possible effects of the drug so you are prepared Combinations: Consider the interaction of any drugs you plan to mix or may already have in your system Expectations: If you plan to take a drug, what do you think you will feel and get out of it? Motivations: Why are you considering taking psychoactive drugs? Do you feel comfortable with the decision? Alternatives: There are often other ways to have the experience you are looking for without using drugs – have you considered these? Help: Reassure yourself that you know how to get help if you or a friend has a bad experience while taking drugs Frequency: Think about how often you are taking psychoactive drugs and if you may be developing dependency Honesty: Are you honest and transparent about your drug-taking habits with friends and family? Risk: Have you thought about the potential risk factors of taking the drug, and the short- and long-term health concerns?

We have covered a lot about psychoactive drugs in this module, from the biology to the social aspects of recreational drug use. The best way to avoid harm is not to consume psychoactive drugs. However, we hope that you are now better equipped to minimise the harm of recreational drugs if you, or your friends, choose to use them.
