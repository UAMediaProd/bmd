Financial fraud exceeding $489 million occurred in Australia during 2018 (ACCC, 2019); an increase of $149 million from 2017.

Examples of financial fraud include bank card fraud, remote banking fraud and phishing. Bank card fraud is where a person's card details are copied (or stolen) and then used fraudulently without the owner's consent.

Banks and financial institutions are investing huge amounts of money and resources to prevent fraud, but what steps can you take to protect your personal finances?

 Account activity

Check your bank account daily for any unusual activity – contact your bank immediately if there are any transactions you don't recognise.

 Protect passwords and security details

Keep all passwords/banking security details safe and make sure you use different passwords for different accounts.

 Online payments

Use secure online payments for bills – this is safer and easier to track than paying in cash, and you have evidence of the payments.

 Personal information

Destroy any account statements that contain any personal information/account details by shredding them or blacking out any sensitive information, such as your address.

 Be cautious of phone calls from your bank

Banks will not generally call customers out of the blue. Never divulge any personal details/your account number/your PIN code – just hang up! You should also report the call to your bank so they are aware that customers are being targeted by a scam.
