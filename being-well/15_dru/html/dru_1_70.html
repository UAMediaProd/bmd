Let's have a brief recap before finishing the module:

  • Psychoactive drugs alter our perception, mood and behaviour
  • Some psychoactive drugs are legal and some are illegal
  • However, this doesn't necessarily mean legal drugs are safer to consume
  • Psychoactive drugs interact with neurons in our brain, which is why they alter perception, mood and behaviour
  • This imbalance can have negative short-term consequences, as well as serious long-term consequences for habitual use.

Although it is safer for both long- and short-term health to not take psychoactive drugs at all, why might some people use them?

It is not possible to understand the possible effect of a drug unless you take into account the type of drug, the environment and the individual. The same pill can have different effects on two people taking it at the same time, in the same place. Likewise, the same pill can have different effects on the same person at different times and in different contexts. For this reason, it is very important to be aware of these factors that are beyond the substance itself.
