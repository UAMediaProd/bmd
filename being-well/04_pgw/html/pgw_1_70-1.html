Although many students have very positive experiences working with others, there are occasions when exploitation by the academic community and/or supervisors does occur. Some researchers feel embarrassed to discuss exploitation or even feel obliged to allow exploitation to continue – it is normal to feel this way. In these circumstances it is important that you recognise when there is a serious problem and take action quickly to prevent it from getting any worse.

This can feel like a daunting thing to have to do, but any sort of exploitative or bullying behaviour is totally unacceptable and must be addressed. Most universities have codes of conduct and policies governing appropriate behaviour so do check out the information that your university has on this.

 Your context Your context

If you experience bullying or harassment, student services will be able to provide support and advice.

Exploitation of doctoral students is not the norm, but when it does occur it can have a huge impact on the student. The only way to make exploitation absolutely unacceptable is to ensure that it is exposed, and this may involve you making a formal complaint if you are on the receiving end of this behaviour. If you are involved in a complaints procedure, you will need a lot of support so do ensure that you reach out to friends and student services.

 Peer support Peer support

Remember that there is a community of fellow students around you. Do look out for each other and offer support if you think a student is struggling in their relationship with their supervisor.
