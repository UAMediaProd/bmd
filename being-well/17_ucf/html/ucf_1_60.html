 University costs and funding The cost of going to university and the different types of funding that are available to support you. Tuition fees The direct costs of attending university, including teaching and use of the facilities. Domestic students may be able to defer these to HECS-HELP. For international students, payment plans may be available through the university. Extra costs Additional things you might have to pay for related to your course, like special equipment, placements or field trips. Loans A loan of money to help pay for the costs of studying. Domestic students can apply for a government loan to help pay for tuition fees. This money is paid back in instalments once you earn above a certain threshold. You may also be able to apply for a loan from your university or a community assistance programme to assist with living costs. Scholarships, grants and bursaries Extra financial support from your university, often as a result of a particular set of circumstances. These payments do not need to be paid back, no matter how much you earn after graduation. This support is available for both international and domestic students.
