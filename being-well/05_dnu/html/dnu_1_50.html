There are numerous organic compounds that cannot be produced by our bodies that are absolutely crucial for brain function. How do we get these? Through food, of course! It's not surprising that research shows that positive dietary behaviour, such as having regular nutritious meals throughout the day, has a beneficial impact on a student's academic achievements.

 It is well established that foods rich in Omega-3 fatty acids, like fish and nuts, are essential for healthy brain function. Antioxidants are also important for cognition and aiding learning, and can be found in plant-based foods such as fruits, vegetables, grains, nuts and seeds. Some people use energy drinks to remain alert or energetic. These drinks are very high in sugar and caffeine, which impacts appetite and sleep cycles. Drinking energy drinks may give you an initial 'boost', but overconsuming them could lead to irritability, increased anxiety, gastrointestinal issues and other health problems. Studies show that even a few days of eating freshly prepared meals based on vegetables, grains, and fruits, and avoiding highly processed foods, is beneficial to our ability to concentrate, pay attention or memorise information. Diets with a high intake of grains, vegetables, fruit, nuts, dairy, and fish, and a low intake of sugary drinks, have been linked to better brain health, which is a protective factor in age-related cognitive decline.
