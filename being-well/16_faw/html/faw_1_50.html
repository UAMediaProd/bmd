 Getting hold of essential course textbooks Your university library will have some copies of textbooks available, but there will be restrictions on how long you can borrow items. Look out for second-hand options online as this will be much cheaper, as well as in op shops or Facebook buy, sell and swap groups close to the university – it is possible that other students may have donated some of the volumes you need. Household
costs It is a good idea to check the best value utilities (electric, gas, water) on a price comparison site to see if you could lower your monthly bills. If you live with other people, sharing household essentials such as cleaning products is a good strategy, and will save storage space. Cooking together can also be a very cost-effective way to save money on the food shop. Student
discounts Make full use of your Student ID card, as you may be entitled to concession prices on public transport, at cinemas and sporting events, and other local facilities. Always ask if there is a student discount – if you don't ask, you don't get! Most student discounts are at least 10%, so it is worth a shot – especially for high value items. Consider additional sources of income Seek out your university's careers team or students' association for available part-time work, possibly on campus. You could sign up for schemes such as 'mystery shopping' companies, or look at selling unwanted clothes, books or other items online.

Another way to save money is to look at how you do your grocery shopping. Even if you do not currently buy your own food (perhaps you live with family, or in catered accommodation), it can still be a useful exercise to think about your spending. The module titled: Eating well looks at planning healthy meals when living on a student budget.

Consider the list of 10 common items you may add to a weekly shop. Using the website of your local or preferred supermarket, look up the price of a branded version of each item (e.g. Buttercup for the loaf of bread) and record the amount in the 'Branded' column. Then look up the price of the same item in the supermarket's 'own brand' range (e.g. Aldi's sliced bread) and record the amount in the 'own brand' column. The check the 'Grand total' row to see the difference in overall cost.

This overall saving might not seem that impressive as a one-off, but when you consider how this could add up over a month or a year, it could make a real difference to overall expenditure.

Think carefully about where and when you shop – many supermarkets will discount foods at the end of the day. You can also consider sharing some costs with housemates and buying in bulk (this is often cheaper), and possibly taking turns to cook for the whole household to reduce the cost per portion.
