 Physical activity for students' health and wellbeing

Within a typical week, you should aim to undertake:

  • A combination of physical activities that are manageable, enjoyable and include time for rest and recovery
  • Two hours of moderate exercise, such as brisk walking/wheelchair use
  • Half as much high-intensity exercise, like swimming or running
  • Muscle-strengthening exercises.
 Overcoming sedentary behaviour

Combating sedentary behaviour through exercise gives you more energy, which improves your ability to study and perform at university.

 Benefits of physical activity for students

Physical activity offers lots of benefits, including: helping you to meet new people, concentrate for longer, be more resistant to stress and sleep more soundly.

 Breaking down barriers Barriers that prevent some students from exercising include cost, study commitments and a busy personal life. However, there are numerous low-cost or free opportunities (such as walking in the park or dancing with friends) to help you get fit and improve your overall health and wellbeing. Physical activity throughout your university life Maintaining your physical activity needs will be made easier by using a reflective five-step cycle of success (as seen on the screen titled: Being active for the long-term), and constructing a personal action plan.
