Think of the people, places, strategies, thought processes and behaviours that have helped you in the past to increase your confidence, build up your self-esteem, and bounce back when things go wrong. If you are struggling to recognise these, ask yourself:

  • What actions have helped me before?
  • Who has helped me before?
  • In what environments do I feel stronger?

Having completed this module you may also have some new ideas about what might help you in the future.

Build your own 'NATs survival kit' by creating a list of useful activities or strategies to help you build your sense of confidence, self-esteem and resilience. For example, you might want to spend more time in places where you feel truly comfortable or prioritise spending time with people that make you feel good about yourself.

Remember that our confidence, self-esteem and resilience fluctuate dependent on circumstances. While utilising the helpful strategies will not change this variation, it should help you to feel some sense of control that, whatever happens, you have some deliberate, purposeful things to do to help you manage.
