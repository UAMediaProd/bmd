Support services

Lifeline is a national charity that provides access to 24-hour crisis support and suicide prevention services to all Australians experiencing a personal crisis:
https://www.lifeline.org.au/

Suicide Call Back Service is a nationwide service that provides professional 24/7 telephone and online counselling to people who are affected by suicide:
https://www.suicidecallbackservice.org.au/

The Beyond Now suicide safety planning app helps you stay safe if you're experiencing suicidal thoughts, feelings, distress or crisis:
https://www.beyondblue.org.au/get-support/beyondnow-suicide-safety-planning

R U OK? inspires and empowers people to meaningfully connect with those around them and start a conversation with someone who may be struggling with life:
https://www.ruok.org.au/

R U OK? also has an excellent list of national support services for different health and mental health needs:
https://www.ruok.org.au/findhelp

#YouCanTalk is a national suicide prevention campaign led by Australian mental health and suicide prevention organisations. It aims to empower and increase confidence when it comes to talking about suicide:
https://www.lifeinmindaustralia.com.au/youcantalk

Beyond Blue is available 24/7 for advice, referrals and support from a trained mental health professional:
https://www.beyondblue.org.au/get-support/get-immediate-support

If you're trying to improve your own mental health, or support somebody else with mental health issues, try Head to Health. It provides links to trusted Australian online and phone support services, resources and treatment options:
https://headtohealth.gov.au/

A coping plan app developed by an Australian university:
https://apps.apple.com/au/app/my-coping-plan/id1274357944

Healthdirect has a list of mental health helplines to support you in difficult times:
https://www.healthdirect.gov.au/mental-health-helplines
