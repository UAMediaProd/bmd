Student life often involves balancing study, work, exercise and a social life. So, keeping up the energy to do everything is essential. Food provides the energy to fuel all these tasks, so it is important to eat well and to eat regularly. To get the most energy out of your diet, aim to:

  • Have three main meals and a few nutritious snacks every day to sustain your energy levels. Regular eating helps prevent feeling overly hungry, which can lead to overeating
  • Try to include foods from all the food groups in your main meals to balance your intake
  • Choose snacks wisely to boost your nutrition (fruit and vegetables are great, as are grains, nuts and dairy products)
  • Try to avoid too many energy stimulants, such as coffee, energy drinks and sweets, as relying on these for energy is not a sustainable long-term practice. On the contrary, excessive consumption of energy stimulants may contribute to health issues, such as heart problems, anxiety, sleeping problems, or tooth erosion.
