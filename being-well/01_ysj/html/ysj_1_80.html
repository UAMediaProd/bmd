In this module you have considered student life as a journey of changes and challenges. You have identified your own current position on the student journey, and considered some of the emotional challenges you are facing. You have considered the different aspects of personal identity and how your identity might be shaped on the student journey. And you have learnt about a number of strategies and skills for supporting your adaptability and building your sense of confidence and belonging.

Now you have an opportunity to make a list of the ideas and strategies that feel most relevant to you in your current stage on the journey.

Summary

You are much more likely to make changes if you capture your ideas in the form of concrete 'implementation intentions', which identify specific actions to be undertaken in specific contexts. It is also useful to include repetitions of a plan in order to help you build a new habit. For example, "On the first day back at university after the summer holiday I will go to the library to find a good place to set up my study base. I will go back there every day for two weeks, even if it is only for ten minutes."

Or "In week two of semester I will sign up to one of the student clubs to meet new people and engage in events. I will make sure that I contact a member each fortnight through social media to expand my network of friends."
