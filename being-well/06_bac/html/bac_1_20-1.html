Try to take advantage of any green spaces at your university – for example, a sports field that isn't being used, or a campus lake that you can jog around. You can also use the architecture of your university creatively; maybe by running up and down a flight of outdoor steps in a quiet area, or using a bench for press-ups or plank exercises.

Physical activity is available to students in many forms, from recreational exercise to competitive sport. Although we all have different priorities, exercise helps to improve our health and wellbeing in all areas of our life.

Continue on for information about the health and wellbeing benefits of exercise.

 Physical health Physical benefits include a better immune system to fight infection, improved energy and sleep patterns, and maintaining a healthy weight. Adrenalin is released during physical activity, which restricts the blood vessels, increasing blood pressure and glucose levels. As a result, regular exercise contributes to a boost in energy levels. However, it's also important to take the time to relax and recover after exercise so as not to put too much pressure on the body. Psychological wellbeing Exercise generates the release of 'feel good' chemicals (endorphins) in the brain, which lift mood, improve focus and provide a distraction from worries. The release of endorphins also helps to alleviate depression and anxiety, and the chemical dopamine creates feelings of pleasure and reward, which motivate you to repeat a specific behaviour. Social and personal benefits Exercising with others is a great way to strengthen existing relationships, and make new ones. Regular exercise increases testosterone levels, which helps to stimulate a person's libido and therefore their sex drive. Physical activity can also lead to improved perception of self-worth, which enhances our self-esteem.

People who are physically active tend to have more energy, sleep better, have a sharper memory, be more relaxed and have a more positive outlook on life. Physical activity can also be a powerful tool to help combat anxiety and ease the symptoms of depression (for more information on this, please see the module titled: Stress, anxiety and depression).
