Many students find full-time study financially challenging, and without careful budgeting it is easy to get behind with payments. Part-time study

Studying part-time may make it possible for Chloe to carry on working whilst studying, and limit the amount of extra childcare she needs. She may still be eligible for a Centrelink payment and Child Care Subsidy. She will need to budget carefully and account for the fact that she will be studying for a longer period.

Distance learning

Many higher education institutions, as well as Open Universities Australia, offer distance learning options. This provides a great amount of flexibility, allowing students to study at times that fit with their other commitments. In many cases, this means working, studying and family commitments can work alongside one another, whilst limiting some of the other costs of studying, such as commuting and child care. She may still be eligible for a Centrelink payment, so if Chloe chose this option she would need to check and make sure her income would cover her costs.

I'd never had to pay a bill before in my life, so it was a bit of a shock when I found out that I was responsible for paying other bills as well as my rent! The best tip I can give is don't ignore your bills, and don't be afraid to ask for help and advice if you are struggling to pay them.

Mena, 19

Working out how to pay the bills when I moved to a shared house was a bit of a challenge. I had to be really honest about what I could afford and not worry about telling the others when I couldn't afford a payment. We figured out a budget for everyone, so it meant we never fell out over the bills.

Victoria, 21

I got a lot of help from student services. They don't really teach you this stuff in school, but now I'm much better prepared for the future thanks to the advice I've received.

Terrance, 23

Paying the bills always led to massive arguments in our house because we hadn't really thought it through. There was always someone who couldn't pay their share and it put pressure on everyone else. So, we did a bit of research online and took some advice to set aside an equal amount of money each week, and now we use that to pay the shared bills. It has reduced the number of arguments we have!

Billy, 29

We had a kitty for the household items that we shared, like loo paper and cleaning stuff. On the whole that worked well. Some friends had a rota for buying those sorts of items so that no one felt they were always buying for everyone.

Reuben, 20

Think about how this situation could be resolved and what Jenna, Rhian and Henri should do now.

 Although Jenna has genuine concerns about the account – after all, it's in her name – she would be better to discuss this calmly with the others and come up with some solutions together. They will need to discuss how this happened and why they didn't let one another know that there was a problem. Importantly, they need to ensure this is unlikely to happen again, maybe by agreeing to review the account and bills together every month.

Can you think of any solutions to this situation?

It is important that the housemates who have missed their payments top up the account as soon as possible. But Rhian has lost her job and may not be able to do this. Although one of the others might be willing to lend her the money for the time being, this is not necessarily a possibility. Anyway, if Rhian can't pay them back then this could lead to more bad feelings. If a bill can't be paid, it is really important to contact the company concerned and let them know.
