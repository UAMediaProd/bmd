Feeling like you belong to a group is such a basic human need, it is natural to feel anxious about being accepted and 'fitting in'. But a true sense of belonging will only come from connections based on who you really are and what is important to you. The student journey offers wonderful opportunities to develop and grow your own unique identity.

Reflect on the question 'Who are you?' and consider how each of the following areas feeds into your identity:

  • Family and community
  • Cultural identities
  • Social identities
  • Gender identity and expression
  • Sexuality
  • Values, faith or spirituality
  • Personality
  • Personal strengths and challenges
  • Vocational identity
  • Learner/researcher identity

Becoming more aware of the many factors contributing to your own identity is also an opportunity to consider the complex identities of your peers. How can you contribute to an environment where everyone's values and identities are accommodated and celebrated? For example, is there enough variety in your social activities to include peers who don't drink alcohol?

Although some basic categories of our identity are fixed, their meaning and importance – for us and for others – are likely to change over time. Exploring who you are, deepening your understanding of what is important to you and developing your identity are central parts of the student journey.

Keeping your personal values in mind offers you a valuable compass to guide your direction as you travel the journey. This compass is also an important guide for building meaningful connections and relationships with the people around you, which is such an important ingredient in your sense of belonging.
