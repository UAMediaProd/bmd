Lots of distractions will compete for your time as a student. Maintaining a programme of physical activity for a prolonged period of time is not without its difficulties. Therefore, it is important for you to set your physical activity goals and be able to deal with setbacks along the way to achieving them.

The five-step cycle

The five-step cycle is a continuous series of steps that join together to make up your pathway to success. It can be used in all areas of your life, including staying physically active.

Goals

The first step is to set your own goals. This can include goals that are related to achievement, such as keeping to physical activity schedules, or just feeling happier with your level of fitness.

Case study: Jesse is a Master's student who is keen to be more physically active and build their stamina.

Problem

The next step is problem recognition. This means identifying problems that make your goal more difficult to achieve. These may include a lack of motivation to be active, accessibility issues or a lack of support from others.

Case study: As a LGBT+ student, Jesse finds traditional gym environments intimidating. LGBT+ people may be less likely to participate in organised sports due to discrimination and difficulty accessing gender-appropriate exercise spaces.

Analysis

The next step is analysing the root of the problem. This could be poor sleep patterns, time management, or not asking for help when needed. It may also be something out of your control, like being excluded by others.

Case study: Because Jesse feels unwelcome at the local gym, but also doesn't feel comfortable exercising in public (like running in the park), they are confused about what to do next.

Design

The next step is to come up with a plan of action to resolve the problem. This involves using skills that you may have learned to overcome a similar problem in the past. Breaking down a goal into smaller chunks can also be very helpful.

Case study: Jesse has noticed some posters around campus advertising exercise classes that are inclusive and welcoming for LGBT+ students, and decides to enquire with student services about what options are available.

Doing

The final step is putting the plan into action to achieve your physical activity goals, and moving forward. The five-step cycle is part of many future cycles that join together to continue to improve your life.

Case study: Jesse has changed their physical activity expectations, and found options that they find enjoyable and welcoming. This will allow them to reach their goal of improving their level of physical activity and stamina.

The five-step cycle is an effective tool to set physical activity goals and track your progress. It is also a good opportunity to think about any barriers that may be preventing you from exercising to the level you aim to reach, and how these can be overcome.

What are your personal physical activity goals?

This might be something specific, like being able to run a 5k, or something more general, such as improving your overall fitness and stamina levels.

What might make it more difficult for you to achieve your goal?

This could include: not knowing where to start, being unsure of available facilities or not knowing safe techniques for exercising.

What might be the root of the block to your goal?

This could include: personal confidence issues over exercising with others, concerns about going to a class with people in 'better shape' than you, or feeling nervous about using equipment.

What could you do to overcome the barrier you are facing?

A good starting point is to do exercises that are easily available and accessible to you. This could include walking to university, or doing desk exercises in-between study sessions. You can increase the frequency of activity as you find it becoming easier, and start to introduce new forms of exercise. Ask the university sports office about beginners' gym/exercise classes, and don't be afraid to ask friends for advice, or even if they want to join you.

When will you put your plan into action? It helps to set yourself a target!

You can also try using the Action plan template to set short- and mid-term goals. There may be a charity walk or future level of attainment, which is a realistic fitness goal to aspire to.
