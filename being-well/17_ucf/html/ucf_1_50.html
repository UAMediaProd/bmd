One of the biggest differences between HELP loans and commercial loans (like personal loans or payday loans), is that how much you pay back each month is worked out based on how much you earn, rather than how much you owe. Australian students are expected to repay a percentage of any income over the repayment threshold. The more you earn, the higher your repayment will be. Indexation is added to your debt on 1 June each year (see 'Useful links' pod for details).

If you are not eligible for a HELP loan and are using commercial loans to pay your tuition fees, each repayment will be a set amount, usually each week or fortnight, based on the amount borrowed, term of the loan and interest rate.

We've given you these examples to look at to show you how much you might be repaying based on different salaries, but it's also important to remember that you won't need to do any of the maths! Your employer will deduct any repayments directly from your salary and pass it on to the Australian Tax Office (ATO) for you. Also, if your income changes each month, they'll recalculate it each pay day, and if your income drops below the threshold, for any reason at all, you'll simply stop making repayments until you are earning enough again. Some people may never earn enough to pay any of their loan back and that's absolutely fine, it's exactly how the system is meant to work!
