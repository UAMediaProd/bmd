"Hi, how're you going? We haven't talked in ages! Let's catch up for a drink!"

You have probably heard this or something similar before. Have you ever heard people say that "humans are social animals"? Well, alcohol serves as a social lubricant, which combined with our social nature might explain why alcohol is so popular among students, as well as the population more generally. However, more people than ever are now choosing to reduce their levels of drinking, or choosing not to drink at all, particularly adults under 25 years of age (Australian Institute for Health and Welfare, 2017). Universities and students' associations have raised awareness of risky drinking, and there are now more options for social events and interactions that do not involve alcohol.

Why might the team choose to drink alcohol?

They are celebrating a victory. Some people choose to drink alcohol to celebrate a special occasion and enjoy the heightened feelings of happiness and relaxation that alcohol can bring. However, in such situations, it is all too easy to get carried away and drink more than we should.

Why might this couple choose to drink alcohol?

They are enjoying a romantic evening together. In some cuisines, food is often paired with wine that complements the flavour, and it can make the evening feel more special. However, it might not be the best idea to drink alcohol if they are having a 'serious' chat. Alcohol heightens emotions and reduces self-control. Therefore, starting an emotionally charged conversation while drinking alcohol can result in an argument.

Why might this student choose to drink alcohol?

He could be using alcohol to try to feel better about himself or a difficult situation ('drowning his sorrows'). He could also be drinking because he feels bored or lonely. If this becomes a habit, he could develop alcohol dependence and all the negative consequences associated with it. It is best to avoid drinking alone when you are feeling down or negative.

Why might these friends choose to drink alcohol?

They are celebrating the end of exams. It can be a relief to finish the exam season and start the uni break. While it is fun to celebrate by partying with your friends, there are a few things to watch out for. A high percentage of alcohol-related accidents occur after exams are finished. If you've reduced how much you drink during the semester to focus on studying, your tolerance to the effects of alcohol might be very low. Try to keep track of how much you're drinking, and avoid promotions like 2 for 1 drinks or cheap shots. These can make you feel pressure to drink more.

Why might this group of friends choose not to drink alcohol on a night out?

Although it is common to drink alcohol in some bars and clubs, many people choose not to. Maybe they just don't feel like drinking that night, or choose to avoid alcohol altogether. Or perhaps they haven't met up for a while and want to catch up properly without the buzz of alcohol. Some people also prefer to focus on the music rather than drinking, or spend more time dancing instead of lining up at the bar.

Drinking alcohol can make you feel more emotional or behave more aggressively than you would if you were sober.

 True False Alcohol causes emotional volatility, meaning that emotions are heightened. It also reduces inhibitions and self-control, which could lead to a higher chance of someone being involved in an argument or physical fight.

Drinking coffee sobers you up quickly.

 True False Coffee can make you more alert and awake but does not reduce the effects of alcohol. Drinking coffee can make drink-driving especially dangerous due to the impaired judgement and the perceived state of alertness caused by the coffee.

Water does not cancel out the effects of alcohol.

 True False Water or soft drinks do not cancel out the effects of alcohol. However, they do slow down the pace of alcohol intake. Water also combats dehydration, easing the hangover the next morning. It's a good idea to use the 'one on, one off' rule, where you have a glass of water between each alcoholic drink.

A bottle of wine contains the same amount of calories as a burger with fries.

 True False

A bottle of wine or six small bottles of beer contain as many calories as a burger and fries. Alcohol comes from fermenting natural sugar and starch, which is why it contains many calories. These are said to be 'empty calories' because they don't include many nutrients that contribute to your diet.
