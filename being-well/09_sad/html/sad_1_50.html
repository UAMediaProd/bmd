It might be your mood that is the problem for you. If so, there are several things that you can do to help yourself stay well and avoid it dipping too often. It is better to try to do self-care activities little and often, and get into good habits, rather than try to do everything at once.

When we feel down it can be really hard to do nice things for ourselves. This is probably the time we need them most. By taking the time to look after our wellbeing and mood daily, we can sometimes prevent things from deteriorating. We can even help to balance out some of the more difficult feelings.

Tom has been feeling a lack of enthusiasm for his course in recent weeks, and just can't get motivated to do his work. He's also been feeling generally quite 'down' and is finding it hard to sleep and switch off.

What would you advise him to do?

Tom should keep pushing through with his work. He'll feel worse if he starts to fall behind and the enthusiasm is bound to come back. Tom should take breaks from studying and give himself time to relax and decompress from work.

Taking care of yourself

Taking breaks from studying is important for our wellbeing. Although it can feel counter-intuitive to step away from work, it is actually very beneficial, as you will be more relaxed and focused on your studies. There are positive benefits to being outside and experiencing nature. When your mood is stressing you out, go outside and take notice of the world around you. Step 'outside of yourself' for a moment. It really can help. Never underestimate how important sleep routine is. If yours is a bit inconsistent then tackle that first, as it can make a huge difference to how you feel emotionally.

Myra is feeling overwhelmed with work commitments and the breakdown of her romantic relationship. Although she's generally a social person, she is spending all her time alone and ignoring messages. It has now been two or three weeks since she spoke to her friends properly. What would you advise her to do?

Myra is going through a difficult period, so it's a good idea to leave her alone to work through the issues on her own. Myra should be encouraged to try to build up the enthusiasm to interact with other people – cutting herself off will make her feel lonelier and reinforce her low mood.

Social interactions

If you are feeling low, numb or angry, it can really help to share your feelings with someone. It might be hard for you to do this initially, but telling someone you trust how you feel can be a turning point in a challenging situation. Talking to people who care about you is important for your mental health. Being in social situations can help to distract you from difficult personal situations. You could try joining a local class or group to meet people with similar interests to yourself. You may need a little time on your own at first but you should try not to isolate yourself for too long.
