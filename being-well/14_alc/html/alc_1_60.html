 Nurturing a healthy drinking culture 4

There are many things you can do to help create a healthy drinking culture for yourself and others:

  • Check in with yourself and others about how you are feeling before you start drinking
  • Encourage friends not to put themselves at risk when drinking alcohol
  • Keep an eye out for each other and make sure your friends get home safely after a night out.
 Strategies for safer drinking 3

To reduce harm when drinking or planning to drink alcohol, you may want to:

  • Eat before a night out – don't drink on an empty stomach!
  • Monitor how much you are drinking and maintain an awareness about how you are feeling
  • Be aware of how alcohol may interact with any medication (or drugs) you have taken
  • There are no known cures for a hangover other than drinking less alcohol, but drinking water, sleeping well and eating nutritious food may help relieve symptoms.
 Negative effects of drinking alcohol 2

As well as hangovers the day after drinking, there are other negative effects of drinking alcohol:

  • Since alcohol lowers inhibitions and reduces coordination, binge drinking may result in short-term risks such as accidents, injuries, and embarrassing and regrettable decisions
  • Frequent alcohol use can lead to alcohol dependence
  • Drinking alcohol regularly can pose a risk to health in the long-term.
 Why people may choose to drink alcohol 1

There are many reasons why someone may choose to drink alcohol:

  • Some people choose to drink alcohol to relax after a stressful day
  • Drinking alcohol is often seen as part of a celebration, or to help people feel more social
  • People may drink alcohol to dampen negative emotions or to self-medicate a pre-existing mental health disorder.

Try to keep in mind what we have covered in this module and use the information to contribute to a positive drinking culture.
