Useful apps for mental health issues

ReachOut maintains a library of mental health focused apps that address topics like self-harm, meditation, anxiety, helping friends, dealing with breakups, mood tracking and lots more:
https://au.reachout.com/tools-and-apps

Victoria Health did a review of apps to find ones useful for mental health:
https://www.vichealth.vic.gov.au/media-and-resources/vichealth-apps/healthy-living-apps?q=&category=mental%20wellbeing

PsyberGuide is a non-profit organisation with a mission to help consumers choose better mobile apps to support their mental health:
https://psyberguide.org/

Finally, the NHS (National Health Service in the UK) maintains an app library for recommended and approved apps, including mental health:
https://www.nhs.uk/apps-library/filter/?categories=Mental%20health

Useful Australian websites for mental health and related issues

Beacon

A portal to online applications for mental and physical health conditions:
https://beacon.anu.edu.au/

Head to Health

Helping individuals find the right digital mental health resources for their mental health needs:
https://headtohealth.gov.au/

Beyond Blue

Information and support for Australians coping with anxiety or depression:
https://www.beyondblue.org.au/

Centre for Clinical Interventions

A Western Australian psychology practice that provides self-help resources for a range of mental health problems:
https://www.cci.health.wa.gov.au/Resources/Looking-After-Yourself

Headspace

Australia's leading support service for young people aged 12-25:
https://headspace.org.au/

Also check out ReachOut:
https://au.reachout.com/

MindSpot

Get free online assessment and counselling for anxiety, depression and other mental health and stress-related conditions:
https://mindspot.org.au/

Also check out This Way Up:
https://thiswayup.org.au/

And Mental Health Online:
https://www.mentalhealthonline.org.au/

SANE Australia

Supporting the mental health of Australians affected by complex mental illness:
https://www.sane.org/

HealthTalk Australia

Get information about various health-related issues by seeing and listening to the real life experiences of people from a range of backgrounds:
https://healthtalkaustralia.org/

eMHprac

Although originally designed for health professionals, the eMHprac directory contains a comprehensive overview of the many apps, online programs, online forums and phone services that people can use for their mental health:
https://www.emhprac.org.au/directory/

Self-harm and suicide

24/7 telephone and online crisis support and suicide prevention:
https://www.lifeline.org.au/

24/7 telephone and online counselling for those affected by suicide and self-harm:
https://www.suicidecallbackservice.org.au/

The Beyond Now suicide safety planning app helps you stay safe if you're experiencing suicidal thoughts, feelings, distress or crisis:
https://www.beyondblue.org.au/get-support/beyondnow-suicide-safety-planning

A suicide safety planning app that can help to keep you safe when you are feeling low or experiencing suicidal thoughts:
https://www.suicidecallbackservice.org.au/resource/download-reminder-suicide-safety-plan-app/

Websites for Indigenous students

MindSpot Indigenous Wellbeing Course
The Indigenous Wellbeing Course is designed to help Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander adults aged 18 years and over to learn to manage mild, moderate and severe symptoms of depression and anxiety:
https://mindspot.org.au/indigenous-wellbeing

Beyond Blue
Mental health resources and information for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students:
https://www.beyondblue.org.au/who-does-it-affect/aboriginal-and-torres-strait-islander-people
