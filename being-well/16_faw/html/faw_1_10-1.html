Download a glossary of terms used in this programme.

As a student, possibly being away from home or dealing with loans for the first time, you will have a lot of financial considerations. Some students will also face a change in their financial situation as they move through university, and will need to develop a plan to ensure that any existing financial commitments can be honoured.

The aim of this module is to introduce some key ideas to improve your financial awareness, and help to develop strategies to manage finances at university and beyond.

If eligible, I will automatically receive my Centrelink Start-Up Loan at the commencement of each semester.

Incorrect.

Students who receive a Centrelink student payment must apply for the loan through their Centrelink online account in myGov (a secure way to access government online services). You need to select the period for which you wish to receive the loan and submit the application. Students who are not eligible for Centrelink should check if the university has other potential funding options available, e.g. university loans, external not-for-profit loans, or charities that may support students.

Correct.

Students who receive a Centrelink student payment can voluntarily receive a loan at the commencement of the first and second semesters. This loan is repaid through the Australian Taxation Office (ATO), similar to your HELP loan.

I will automatically get an overdraft with my bank account.

Incorrect.

This will need to be applied for and agreed by your bank. The amount will vary depending on your circumstances and is not guaranteed. To be eligible you will need to be 18 years or older and either an Australian citizen or a permanent resident of Australia.

Correct.

This will need to be applied for and agreed by your bank. The amount will vary depending on your circumstances and is not guaranteed. To be eligible you will need to be 18 years or older and either an Australian citizen or a permanent resident of Australia.

As a full-time student living in university accommodation, I won't pay utilities.

Correct.

Most residential colleges or university accommodation fees include all utilities (electricity, gas, water). If you are living in private rental accommodation you will need to pay utilities in addition to your rent.

Incorrect.

Most residential colleges or university accommodation fees include all utilities (electricity, gas, water). If you are living in private rental accommodation you will need to pay utilities in addition to your rent.

I won't pay tax on my part-time job earning $10,000 a year.

Correct.

You won't pay tax on earnings up to the tax-free threshold of $18,200 (19/20) if you are an Australian resident for tax purposes. Any income above the threshold is taxed dependant on which tax bracket your income is in. For example, if you earn $25,000, you would pay 19% tax on any income above $18,200. International students are taxed at the same rate as Australian residents.

My FEE-HELP and/or HECS-HELP loan will be set up automatically.

Incorrect.

To get a FEE-HELP and/or HECS-HELP loan, you will need a tax file number (TFN) and a 'Request for FEE-HELP' application form, which your university provider will give you. Your form must be filled in and submitted to your provider before the census date (or administrative date).

I won't be charged interest on my HELP loan.

Incorrect.

Although there is no interest charged on your HELP debts, indexation is added to your debt on 1 June each year. This means that your debt is adjusted in line with changes in the cost of living. In 2019, HELP debts were indexed by 1.8%.

Correct.

There is no interest charged on your HELP debts; however, indexation is added to your debt on 1 June each year. This means that your debt is adjusted in line with changes in the cost of living. In 2019, HELP debts were indexed by 1.8%.

Most universities have grants, bursaries and scholarships that can be applied for.

Correct.

Grants, bursaries and scholarships are pots of money you can use to pay for studying or living costs, and you never have to pay back. But not everyone will be eligible for them, and some are specific to your university or situation. The module titled: University costs and funding looks at bursaries in more detail. If you are an international student, your university may offer a payment plan or allow you to pay your fees in instalments.

As an international student I will need to set up an Australian bank account whilst at university.

Correct.

Most Australian banks will offer international students bank accounts. Many will allow you to open a bank account online before you arrive in Australia, although you will not be able to withdraw money until you attend a branch and complete an identification check. Your bank will advise what you need to bring, but generally you will need your passport, student ID or proof of enrolment, and proof of your Australian residential address.
